Technical Proficiency



What is the overall quality of your code like?

★ ★ ★

I initially thought it was OK. I wrote the majority of the Bootstrap, and spent a lot of time going through each of the forms making sure they were accurate. I tried hard to keep them streamlined and formatted nicely. The problem I had was when the forms were validated. They all failed miserably. I didn't even know about HTML validation, but I do now!! The forms were then of course...validated.




How well did you follow best practices in development?

★ ★

If I'm honest I didn't. At least not at the start. This was mainly due to the fact that I didn't even know what the best practices were, and I was really only concentrating on producing code. It wasn't until the validation shenanigans mentioned above that I thought I should take a serious look at it.




How well did you use appropriate version control?

★ ★ ★ ★

I was a committed committer to GIT. I tended to push my changes as soon as I had finished a change to a form. This was largely driven out of the fear of either; someone pulling anything other than the most recent version which meant possibly having to deal with some sort of unusual merge situation, or something happening to my local copy and losing my changes.
