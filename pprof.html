How often do you attend scheduled group meetings/scrums?

★ ★ ★ ★

I attended the bulk of the meetings. The meetings that I didn't attend I had a genuine reason not to. Working full time means I can't always get away if something crops up at work and I have to deal with it. Any meetings I didn't attend I made sure to touch base with the group and find out what I had missed, and what needed to be done.




How well did you communicate with others in your group?

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Our slack channel was our main form of communication during the project. I used it a lot, and was concious of the importance to "stay in touch" with the group. I used Slack to share code snippets and client correspondence, arrange meetings, praise and encourage the other group members, and just chat about various aspects of the project. I also found it useful for experimenting with, and honing my emoji use. By the end of the project I found I had used emojis that I had previously only dreamed of using in a actual conversation. How well did you document your work throughout the project?

★

I wasn't great at documenting anything. At the beginning I wasn't even commenting the HTML until I saw what Bettina was doing. After seeing that I made more of an effort to comment my work, and it certainly helped later on with things like navigating the pages and identifying script functions.




How well did you respond to problems or changing requirements?

★ ★ ★ ★

This project had a lot of twists and turns. Firstly because there were so many forms, all with slightly different wording, or requirements. Regularly we would come across things we were unsure of, or things we had assumed that turned out to be not the case at all once we had clarified with Derek. There were also obvious differences between storing data on a piece of paper compared to a database. We often had to add additional form elements to correctly store data after receiving feedback from Derek. For my part, making changes all the time didn't really bother me, and I think as a group we handled these changes very well.
